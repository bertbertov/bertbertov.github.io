Hanzi Drill

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 May 2026 · Developer: Albert Kamalov · Contact: bertbertov@gmail.com

Summary

Hanzi Drill collects no personal data. Everything stays on your device. No accounts, no cloud sync, no ads, no analytics, no telemetry.

What data Hanzi Drill handles

  • The 30-character HSK 1 deck is bundled inside the app.
  • Your finger-trace strokes are held only in memory while you are on the drill screen and are discarded when you tap “Next character”.
  • Nothing is saved between sessions, nothing is uploaded.

Permissions used

Hanzi Drill requests no runtime permissions.

What we never collect

  • Your name, email, phone number, or any other identifier.
  • Your location (unless explicitly noted in “Permissions used” above).
  • Device identifiers, advertising IDs, or anything that could uniquely identify you.
  • Crash reports, usage analytics, behavioral telemetry — none of it.

Third parties

Hanzi Drill contains no third-party SDKs that transmit data. There are no analytics providers, no ad networks, no crash reporters, no remote configuration services.

Children

Hanzi Drill is not directed at children under 13. Since the app collects no personal data, no special protections under COPPA / GDPR-K are required, but you should still use your own judgment about whether this app is appropriate for the child in your care.

Your data, your control

All data Hanzi Drill stores lives in a local database on your device. To delete everything: uninstall the app. Android removes the local database when the app is uninstalled.

If you have Google Drive automatic backup enabled in Android Settings, an encrypted copy of the app’s local data may be included in your system-level Google backup. That backup is controlled by Google’s privacy policy, not by Hanzi Drill, and is restored only if you reinstall on a new device while signed in to the same Google account.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the “Last updated” date above will change. Material changes (e.g. if a future version adds optional cloud sync) will be disclosed in the app’s release notes on Google Play before the changed version ships.
