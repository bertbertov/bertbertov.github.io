Privacy Policy

Last updated: 19 May 2026 · Developer: Albert Kamalov · Contact: bertbertov@gmail.com

Summary

Stutter collects no personal data. Everything stays on your device. No accounts, no cloud sync, no ads, no analytics, no telemetry.

What data Stutter handles

  • While a focus session is active, Stutter holds the task name and timer settings you entered in memory and in a persistent on-device notification so you can end the session at any time.
  • When you end the session (or the timer reaches zero), this state is discarded.
  • No session history is recorded. No data leaves your device.

Permissions used

  • POST_NOTIFICATIONS — required to show the persistent “focus session in progress” notification with an End-session action.
  • FOREGROUND_SERVICE & FOREGROUND_SERVICE_SPECIAL_USE — required to keep the timer and periodic voice check-ins running while the screen is off. The foreground service is terminated automatically when the session ends or you tap End in the notification.
  • TextToSpeech — Stutter uses Android’s built-in TextToSpeech engine to speak the check-in phrase aloud. The text is generated on-device; no audio is uploaded.

What we never collect

  • Your name, email, phone number, or any other identifier.
  • Your location (unless explicitly noted in “Permissions used” above).
  • Device identifiers, advertising IDs, or anything that could uniquely identify you.
  • Crash reports, usage analytics, behavioral telemetry — none of it.

Third parties

Stutter contains no third-party SDKs that transmit data. There are no analytics providers, no ad networks, no crash reporters, no remote configuration services.

Children

Stutter is not directed at children under 13. Since the app collects no personal data, no special protections under COPPA / GDPR-K are required, but you should still use your own judgment about whether this app is appropriate for the child in your care.

Your data, your control

All data Stutter stores lives in a local database on your device. To delete everything: uninstall the app. Android removes the local database when the app is uninstalled.

If you have Google Drive automatic backup enabled in Android Settings, an encrypted copy of the app’s local data may be included in your system-level Google backup. That backup is controlled by Google’s privacy policy, not by Stutter, and is restored only if you reinstall on a new device while signed in to the same Google account.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the “Last updated” date above will change. Material changes (e.g. if a future version adds optional cloud sync) will be disclosed in the app’s release notes on Google Play before the changed version ships.
